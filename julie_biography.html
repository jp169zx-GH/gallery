Li Chen Ma:

A Journey of Self-Discovery Through Paint

Li Chen Ma’s path to becoming an oil painter defied convention from the very beginning. At 46, when many artists have already established their careers, she picked up her first brush—not in a formal studio, but in the quiet solitude of self-directed learning.

What began as humble attempts at copying masterworks soon blossomed into something far more personal: a visual language entirely her own. “As long as I feel it is beautiful, there should be no rules for painting,”

She (Li Chen Ma) asserts, a philosophy that permeates every canvas she creates. This fearless approach to art-making, unburdened by academic constraints, has become her signature.

Her artistic foundation, though unconventional, proved pivotal. While studying at San Diego State University in California, Ma took art courses that would plant the seeds of her creative vision.

One assignment—transforming everyday objects into creatures—captured her imaginative spirit perfectly. Her drawing of a broom metamorphosing into a cat so impressed her instructor that it was selected for the student outdoor gallery, an early validation of her unique perspective.

Years later, upon returning to California, Ma’s artistic interests expanded to embrace landscape and interior design. This evolution is reflected in her work today, where serene landscapes and contemplative floral still life dominate her portfolio. Yet her paintings resist easy categorization—they are neither strictly realist nor impressionist, but rather exist in a liminal space where creativity and artistry merge to reveal her distinctive worldview.

A profound sense of tranquility suffuses Ma’s work, drawing from her deep Buddhist faith. Her art embodies her life philosophy: the delicate balance between yin and yang, light and shadow, stillness and movement. In each brushstroke, she invites viewers into a world where beauty transcends rules and artistic freedom becomes a form of meditation.
